Title: Phase II Clinical Trial of Treatment X in Patients with Condition Y

Study Design:
This was a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase II clinical trial
evaluating the safety and efficacy of Treatment X in adult patients diagnosed with Condition Y.
A total of 120 participants were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to receive either Treatment X or placebo
for 12 weeks.

Inclusion Criteria:
- Adults aged 18–75 years with a confirmed diagnosis of Condition Y.
- Stable background therapy for at least 8 weeks prior to screening.

Exclusion Criteria:
- History of severe hypersensitivity reactions to components of Treatment X.
- Significant hepatic or renal impairment (AST/ALT > 3x ULN, eGFR < 40 mL/min/1.73 m²).

Primary Endpoint:
The primary endpoint was the change from baseline to Week 12 in Symptom Severity Score (SSS),
measured using a validated 0–100 scale, where higher scores indicate more severe symptoms.

Secondary Endpoints:
- Proportion of patients achieving at least a 30% reduction in SSS at Week 12.
- Change from baseline to Week 12 in quality-of-life score (QOL-12 questionnaire).
- Incidence of treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs).

Efficacy Results:
Patients receiving Treatment X showed a statistically significant reduction in SSS compared with placebo.
The mean change from baseline to Week 12 in the Treatment X group was -24.3 (SD 10.5) versus -11.2 (SD 9.8)
in the placebo group (p < 0.001).

Safety and Tolerability:
Treatment X was generally well tolerated. The most commonly reported side effects of Treatment X were:
- Mild headache (18% of patients)
- Nausea (12% of patients)
- Transient dizziness (9% of patients)

Serious adverse events were rare and occurred in 2 patients (3%) in the Treatment X group and 3 patients (5%)
in the placebo group. None of the serious adverse events were assessed as related to Treatment X by the
investigators.

Conclusions:
Treatment X demonstrated clinically meaningful improvements in symptom severity and was generally well tolerated
in adults with Condition Y over a 12-week treatment period.
